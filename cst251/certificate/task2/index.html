Alice Paul


Alice Paul's accomplishments and fun facts:

  • Alice Paul was born in 1885

  • Was apart of the Quakers

  • Attended Swarthmore College

  • Returned to the US in 1910 to advocate for women's rights

  • Became a leader in the suffragist movement

  • Earned a Ph.D. from the University of Pennsylvania in 1912

  • Created National Woman's Party with Lucy Burns

  • Successfully passed the 19th Amendment in 1920

  • In 1923 she introduced the first Equal Rights Amendment in Congress

  • Later on advocated for the Equal Right's Amendment


There will never be a new world order until women are a part of it.



- Alice Paul
